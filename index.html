summer.

violet infused goat milk
washes away the birch's tears.
white linen turns cream on olive skin.
I swim in between the blank spaces of sticky watermelon parchment;
ant-like seeds fly behind your shirt.
I spit, birds chirp, you growl;
bend my pussywillow twigs and fight the drought.
coarse is the soil this summer;
we pour extra virgin olive oil in every crevice
to aid and nourish the land of ripe tomatoes.
