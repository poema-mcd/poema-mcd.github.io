the lamb.

did we not feed them well?

a four-legged skeleton lies in my lap,
so famished you barely see a curl.

did we not sweat for hay and water?

gentle wheezes;
calls into the clouds.

did we not make sure they are mature to be mothers?

each breath seems hours apart;
barely noticeable how its fragile ribs stretch.

did we not give them wide pastures of green?

its body, one could carry in a palm,
shakes like the twigs of aspen trees.
